Example - Multi-Level Supply Chain

In this example, we'll show a multi-level supply chain with a "grandparent" scenario, where:

  • External suppliers source a primary DC (DC-North America).
  • The primary DC sources regional DCs (DC-US East and DC-US West).
  • The regional DCs source their local stores.

Workflow

Click to view a diagram of a multi-level supply chain.

Instructions

  1. From the Maintain tab, click Supply Chain.

  2. In the Open Supply Chain dialog box, select [Supplier - DC/Store - Store].

    Note: If the Open Supply Chain dialog box does not automatically appear, click Open Supply Chain on the toolbar.

  3. The supply chain appears in the Supply Chain screen. In the Hierarchy pane, note that all of the stores are children of their respective DCs.

  4. In the Hierarchy pane, select DC - North America. In the Alternate Source pane, note that it's primary source is External Supplier.

  5. In the Hierarchy pane, expand DC - North America and select DC - US East. In the Alternate Source pane, note that it's primary source is DC - North America.

  6. In the Hierarchy pane, expand DC - US East and select the Atlanta store. In the Alternate Source pane, note that it's primary source is DC - US East.

Results

With this supply chain, JustEnough will:

  1. Calculate projected demand versus projected stock on hand for each item at each store.
  2. Roll up store demand to calculate stock needed at each regional DC.
  3. Roll up regional DC demand to calculate stock needed at primary DCs.
  4. Generate sufficient purchase orders from suppliers to ensure enough stock is available at primary DCs to distribute to regional DCs as needed.
  5. Generate distribution orders between primary DCs, regional DCs, and stores to ensure stores have enough stock on hand to meet anticipated demand.
