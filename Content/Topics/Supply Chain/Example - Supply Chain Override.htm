Example - Supply Chain Override

In this example, we'll create an override that tells JustEnough to ignore the designed supply chain for a specific set of items at specific sites, and instead use another source for those items.

Setup

The following objects are used in this example.

  • Supply Chain BC 1 - SC2 [Supplier - DC - Store]
  • Filter BC1 - SC2 (Item Filter for SC2 Override)
  • Filter BC1 - SC2 (Site Filter for SC2 Override)

Instructions

  1. From the Maintain tab, click Supply Chain.

  2. In the Open Supply Chain dialog box, select the supply chain you want to create an override for.

    For this example, select "Supply chain BC1 - 1 SC1 [Supplier - DC- Store]".

  3. In the Override pane, click New on the Override toolbar. A new override row appears in the pane.

  4. Click the '...' button in the new override's Item Filter column to select the items that will be included in the override.

  5. In the Items filter, choose BC1 - SC2 (Item Filter for SC2 Override).

  6. Click the '...' button in the new override's Site Filter column to select the sites that will be included in the override.

  7. In the Items filter, choose choose BC1 - SC2 (Site Filter for SC2 Override).

  8. In the Override Source pane, click New.A new override source row appears in the pane.

  9. In the new source's Source Supplier list, choose JustEnough JustinTime as the override supplier.

  10. In the new source's Lead Time Days column, enter the value 7.
  11. Click Save on the Supply Chain toolbar to save the new override to the supply chain.
  12. Click Reprocess in the Supply Chain toolbar to process the saved changes and rebuild the supply chain.

Results

For the sites specified in the override (step 6 above), the override items (step 4 above) will now be sourced from JustEnough JustinTime.
