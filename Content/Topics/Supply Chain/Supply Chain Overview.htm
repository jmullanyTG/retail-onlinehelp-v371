Supply Chain Overview

JustEnough's Supply Chain feature lets you define the supply chains that provide inventory to your distribution centers (DCs) and stores.

ERP data integration

JustEnough uses supplier and site data from your ERP system as the building blocks for your supply chains. You can view this site data in several places within JustEnough:

  • Supplier Manager - displays data about your external suppliers.
  • Site Manager - displays data about your stores and DCs.
  • Item Manager - displays the supplier for an item at a location.

Flexible supply chain designs

Real-world supply chains can be simple or complex, and JustEnough provides you with the flexibility to design supply chains that reflect this reality.

Supplier to DC/Store supply chains

In these "flat" supply chains, stores get their inventory directly from external suppliers.

Supplier to DC to Store supply chains

This is a traditional supply chain design, with external suppliers feeding distribution centers (DCs) which in turn distribute inventory to local stores.

  • See example

Multi-level supply chains

This design has multiple levels of distribution centers (primary and regional) supplying stores.

  • See example

Redistribution supply chains

This is a special type of supply chain which lets you group stores or DCs into redistribution pools. These pools define where inventory can be redistributed from when there are inventory overages or shortages in your sites. You can customize lead times and source priorities within each pool as needed.

  • See example

Supply chain overrides

Supply chain overrides are used for situations when you want certain products at certain sites to be supplied from a different source than their regular supply chain. For example, during November and December you may want suppliers to deliver Christmas-related merchandise directly to stores instead of your DCs. Overrides can be planned for specific time periods (such as holiday rushes) or set to run indefinitely.

  • See example

Customizable source priority

If a site is facing a potential shortfall in inventory, you can rank the preferred order of other sites it receives stock from. For example, you may want to prioritize geographically "close" sites but protect your flagship stores. To determine site priority, JustEnough checks the following values (in order):

  1. Priority defined for the site in the supply chain.
  2. Priority group (if present).
  3. Global site priority.
  4. Site ID.
  5. Wait time days.

Automated supply chain resolution

You can create a task in JustEnough's Job Manager to re-process your supply chains as part of a nightly or weekly job. This ensures that any additions or modifications made to your supply chains get propagated throughout the system.
