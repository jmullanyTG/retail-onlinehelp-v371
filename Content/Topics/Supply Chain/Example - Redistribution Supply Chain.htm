Example - Redistribution Supply Chain

Redistribution supply chains create site pools which define where inventory can be redistributed from when there are overages or shortages in your stores or DCs.

In this example, we'll assign the site "Web-Canada" to a pool of Canadian stores and adjust lead times and source priorities accordingly within the pool.

Instructions

  1. From the Maintain tab, click Supply Chain.

  2. In the Open Supply Chain dialog box, select the [Redistribution ] supply chain.

    Note: If the Open Supply Chain dialog box does not automatically appear, click Open Supply Chain on the toolbar.

  3. The supply chain appears in the Supply Chain screen. Note that the Hierarchy pane contains an Unassigned Sites list and a set of 7 site pools. In this supply chain, a separate pool has been created for each site region. Each pool contains the stores from its region.

  4. To add unassigned sites to a pool, simply drag the stie from the Unassigned Sites list and drop it into a pool. For this example, drag the site Web-Canada into the Canada pool and then click Save in the Supply Chain toolbar.

  5. Click on any site in the Canada pool to view the pool details, which include a lead time matrix for sites within the pool.

  6. The Web-Canada site inherits the pool's default lead time value (2 days). You can customize lead time within the grid by clicking the LeadTime value for any site-site combination and entering a new value. In this example, we'll change the lead time between Web-Canada and Vancouver from 2 days to 3 days.

  7. Finally, we want the Ottawa store to get inventory from the Web-Canada site before any other site in the pool. To set this priority, go to the Ottawa row in the matrix and enter 1 in the priority column under Web-Canada.

  8. Click Save in the Supply Chain toolbar to save the changes.
