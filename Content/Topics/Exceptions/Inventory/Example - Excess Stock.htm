Example - Excess Stock

Excess stock exceptions identify items that are projected to be overstocked at a site beyond specified day and value thresholds. In such cases, you may want to redistribute the excess stock to other locations.

You can use filters and views to create an ad-hoc Excess Stock report in Item Manager that displays your excess stock exceptions. In this example, we'll use a pre-created exception filter that identifies items with 60 days of excess stock and $5,000 in excess stock value.

Instructions

  1. From the Manage tab, click Item Manager.

  2. In the Items dialog box, select the Inventory Exception: Excess For Redistribution filter.

  3. In the Choose Fields dialog box, select the Inventory Exception: Overstock (Excess) view.

  4. In the item document, you can see all of the items that exceed the Excess Stock Days and Excess Stock Value thresholds specified in the filter.

    For this example, the threshold is "more or equal to 60 days".

  5. In the grid, you can sort items by priority ("A" items listed first) and review each item's Excess Stock Days, Excess Stock Quantity, Excess Stock Value, and more.

  6. In the Details pane, review an item's Stock Holding Projection tab to see the projected amount of excess stock at the location over time.

  7. Review other tabs in the Detail pane as desired to get a better understanding of the forecasted demand, inventory policy, and other information governing the item stock at the location.

Potential Actions

Actions you can take to resolve overstock exceptions include:

  • Redistributing excess stock.
  • Canceling or deferring upcoming orders until stock quantities are balanced with demand.
