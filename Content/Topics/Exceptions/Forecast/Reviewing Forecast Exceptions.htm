Reviewing Forecast Exceptions

The process for reviewing forecast exceptions is straightforward. For each forecast, determine if the forecast needs to be altered. If so, pick a method to alter the forecast and, when done, regenerate the forecast. If the revised forecast is acceptable, mark it as reviewed and continue to the next forecast in need of review.

Workflow

Click open the Managing Forecast Exceptions workflow.

Reviewing forecasts

  1. From the Manage tab, click Forecasts.

  2. Choose the level or inventory filter that corresponds to the exception type(s) you want to review.

  3. A forecast document opens containing the items with forecasts that match the filtered exception type.

  4. Review the forecast and demand history to determine whether the currently displayed forecast is acceptable "as is" or if it should be altered.

    • If you decide to alter the forecast, it is best to do so iteratively. That is, make one change and then regenerate the forecast to review its effect before making any further changes.
    • You can regenerate forecasts manually by clicking Forecast (or Save and Forecast) on the forecast toolbar.
  5. When you are ready to accept the forecast, click Reviewed on the forecast toolbar. This removes the forecast from the current exception filter.

Altering forecasts

The following methods are commonly used to alter forecasts. The specific method that works best for any given forecast may depend on the exception type and the unique circumstances of the item.

  • Selecting a different forecast method.
  • Adjusting the lost sales quantities.
  • Using an item supersession.
  • Managing seasonal trends.
  • Using a causal or event.
  • Altering store-level split.
  • Manually adjusting the forecast.
