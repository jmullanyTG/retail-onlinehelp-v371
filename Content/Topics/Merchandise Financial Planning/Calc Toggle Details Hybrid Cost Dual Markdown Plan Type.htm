Calc Toggle Details – Hybrid Cost Dual Markdown Plan Type

Though many similarities exist between the calc toggles for the Hybrid Cost & Retail plan types and those for the Hybrid Cost Dual Markdown type, there are some major differences that warrant the examples in this section. There are also a few behaviors specific to this plan type worth noting that will help you better understand the way this plan type functions:

  • Markdowns (Promo) and Promo Sales are linked: the nature of a promotional (or temporary) markdown is such that it is only realized when an accompanying sale of a unit on promotion is sold, thus promotional sales must be present in order for there to be promotional markdowns and vice-versa. That said, any time a change to either promo markdowns or promo sales occurs, there must also be an accompanying change to the other metric
  • Note: Markdowns (Promo) values can always be validated via (AUT – Promo AUR) * Promo Sales Units, however, not all significant digits stored in the database are displayed on screen, thus small variances may occur when manually calculating based on on-screen values. Exact values can be calculated by exporting to Excel and using formulas including the relevant metric cells
  • Markdowns (Perm) are only calculated as a percent of Sales: contrary to promotional markdowns, permanent markdowns are realized at the time the price of goods is decreased, regardless of when the marked down units actually sell. That said, Markdown Sales planned could potentially include units that were marked down in the past, and thus, cannot be directly linked to permanent markdowns. They are calculated as a percentage of sales and subtracted from MMU in the period they are realized to assist in gauging true profitability for that period
  • AUT holds unless changed directly or via IMU %: while AUT is directly related to the amount of promotional markdowns in a given period, we never recalculate AUT to achieve changes in markdowns, opting instead to recalculate Promo AUR or Promo Sales Units. This being true, AUT for a period (or other periods beneath an aggregate level lock) will not change unless it is changed directly by the user or indirectly through a change to IMU %
  • Note: AUT at aggregate levels (ie. Month, etc.) may fluctuate without being changed via these methods due to the mix of inventory represented in the levels beneath

Keeping in mind the points above, you will now more readily understand the calc toggle examples that follow:

  • Sales
  • HOLD Markdown % of Sales, AUR, IMU % | Calc Sales Units, COGS, IMU Retail, MMU, Markdowns
  • Example: the default Sales calc toggle, this is employed when the user wishes to change sales revenue and units while maintaining the planned average selling price (AUR) per unit; this toggle is also allows the user to maintain Markdowns % of Sales (Promo)

In the example above, Sales has been changed to $150,000 for 2018_wk10

 

Note that Sales Units has increased to 8,309 while AUR has remained at $18.05 to balance the change in Sales. Additionally, Markdowns (Promo) and Markdowns (Perm) have increased to $22,500, and $15,000, respectively, to balance their corresponding Markdowns % to Sales metrics, which have held

 

  • HOLD Sales Units, COGS, IMU, Markdowns % of Sales (Perm) | Calc AUR, MMU, Markdowns, Markdowns % of Sales (Promo)
  • Example: the alternate calc toggle for Sales allows for a slightly different approach in which the user wishes to maintain Sales Units while adjusting the average selling price (AUR) per unit; note that Markdowns % of Sales (Perm) still holds but Markdowns % of Sales (Promo) cannot

In the example above, Sales has now been decreased to $75,000 for 2018_wk10

 

This time, Sales Units holds at 5,540 while AUR decreases to $13.54 to balance the change in Sales. Markdowns % of Sales (Perm) has held at 10%, just like in the first toggle’s example, but this time, Markdowns % of Sales (Promo) must recalculate along with Markdowns (Promo) and Markdowns (Perm) because the change in AUR widens the gap between AUT and the underlying Promo AUR, forcing an increase in Markdowns (Promo)

  • Example: the default Sales Units calc toggle, this is employed when the user wishes to change sales units with a resulting change to sales revenue, while maintaining the planned average selling price (AUR) per unit; this toggle is also allows the user to maintain Markdowns % of Sales (Promo)

  • Example: the alternate calc toggle for Sales Units allows for a slightly different approach in which the user wishes to maintain Sales while adjusting the average selling price (AUR) per unit; note that Markdowns % of Sales (Perm) still holds but Markdowns % of Sales (Promo) cannot

Additionally, Markdowns (Promo) and Markdowns % of Sales (Promo) must recalculate because the change in AUR shrinks the gap between AUT and the underlying Promo AUR, forcing a decrease in Markdowns (Promo)

  • Example: the alternate calc toggle for AUR allows for a slightly different approach in which the user wishes to maintain sales revenue while adjusting sales units for the period; as with the standard AUR toggle, Markdowns % of Sales (Perm) still holds but Markdowns % of Sales (Promo) cannot

  • IMU % (and AUT)
  • HOLD Sales, Sales Units, AUR, Markdowns (Perm), Markdowns % of Sales (Perm) | CALC AUT, IMU Retail, Markdowns (Promo), Markdowns % of Sales (Promo)
  • Example: the single IMU % calc toggle in this plan type allows users to adjust ticket price and promotional markdowns by setting target initial profitability percent

In the example above, IMU % has been increased to 80% for 2018_wk10

 

Note that AUT has increased to $26.46 to balance the increase in initial profit potential, however, because AUR has held, this also widens the gap between AUT and the underlying Promo AUR, thus forcing an increase in Markdowns (Promo). Because Sales has held, Markdowns % of Sales (Promo) must increase to balance the rise in Markdowns (Promo), however, Markdowns (Perm), being defined solely as a % of Sales maintains at $10,000

  • Note: because changing AUT directly always yields a single behavior in each plan type, AUT is not listed explicitly in the Calculation Toggles tab, however, changing AUT essentially functions the same as changing IMU % but reciprocal – so changing AUT calculates IMU %. All other behavior described in the above example is the same.
  • Markdowns (Perm)
  • HOLD Sales, Sales Units, AUR, COGS, IMU | Calc MMU, Markdowns, Markdown % of Sales
  • Example: the single Markdowns (Perm) calc toggle in this plan type allows users to plan permanent markdowns via direct value entry or as a % of sales and see the impact on profitability without adjusting sales

  • Markdowns (Promo)
