Causals Overview

Causals tell JustEnough when specific periods of increased demand for your items should not be used to forecast normal demand.

For example, when a promotion is run on an item, demand typically increases. However, if JustEnough's forecasting engine is not aware that a promotion ran during the period of increased demand, it interprets the increase as an increase in "normal" demand and will forecast higher future demand for the item accordingly. To prevent this from happening, you can use a causal to tell JustEnough that the increased demand was caused by the promotion. JustEnough then knows not to count that increased demand as "normal" when it generates forecasts for the item.

There are four types of causals which can be created in the JustEnough system:

  • (normal) Causal
  • Manual Causal
  • Event
  • Informational Causal

For descriptions of each causal type, see General Information.
