The first step is to calculate some statistical properties for the item’s demand that will be used in the Monte Carlo simulation.

Property

Description

Intermittency

A measure of how consistent an item’s sales are, what percentage of periods in a given time frame can one expect zero sales

Average Non-Zero Demand

The average expected demand per period, only measured across  periods with non-zero demand

Standard Deviation Non-Zero Demand

The standard deviation of demand across  periods with non-zero demand

The next step is using the simulated demand distribution to calculate an overall demand satisfaction level.  The demand satisfaction is dependent on total demand which can vary greatly as can be seen from the above distribution.

We will choose an appropriate level of demand based on a certain level of confidence, for this specific distribution we can say with 50% confidence that the total demand will be 35 units or less, or at an 80% confidence level that the total demand will be 46 units or less.

The confidence level is dynamically calculated for each item based on how volatile the demand is, and how many observations the demand statistics are based on.  A very volatile item, or an item with only 2 periods of history will have a very conservative confidence level, where as a high volume, more predictable item, will have a much higher confidence level.

Based on the final confidence level the appropriate total demand is then selected from the Monte Carlo distribution, total demand satisfied is calculated, and our forecast is adjusted to a level where the total demand satisfied percentage is reached.

In the special case where all periods of historic demand are equal to zero, the forecast will naturally be trended downwards to zero (downward re-trend will be restricted by the min re-trend factor setting).

Because the majority of items are very intermittent, this situation can occur quite often especially in the early stages where an item does not have a lot of history. 

To ensure that we do not re-trend downwards too aggressively where not a lot of observations are available, a confidence level will not be used, instead Estimated demand will be set equal to a linear combination of Total forecast,
