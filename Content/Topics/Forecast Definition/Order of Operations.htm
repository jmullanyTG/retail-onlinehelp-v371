Order of Operations

The Forecast Definition is bound within a specific and linear order of operations, the understanding of which is necessary to any successful analysis of a particular forecast.

The order of operations for a forecast definition is a linear process which contains 11 individual steps. Some steps may be left blank if it is determined that they are not relevant to a specific forecast. They are:

  1. Pre-Survey
  2. Pre-Rule (a series rule)
  3. Pre-Processor
  4. Mid-Pre-Rule (a series rule)
  5. Backtrack
  6. Simulation
  7. Forecast
  8. Mid-Post-Rule (a series rule)
  9. Post-Processor
  10. Post-Rule (a series rule)
  11. Post-Survey

“Pre” and “Post” in this instance refer to the Backtrack-Simulation-Forecast steps, which generate the actual forecast.
