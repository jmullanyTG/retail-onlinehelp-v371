Data Cleansing Steps

System will begin with Raw Sales Data based on the scenario inputs:

Note: prior to data cleansing, the system must aggregate sales data from inventory (intersection of site and item master/size)/day level to variant/cluster/period level; to do this, the system first sums the data to variant/site/period, then takes an average of all resulting data points to seed the variant/cluster/period data points used in the steps described in this section

Step 1

To remove any data points that are not indicative of a variants normal sales potential a max sales value is calculated at variant level, within each variant any data point that falls below a specified percentage of that max is eliminated. This is so that extreme outliers don't skew the deseasonalization process.

The % of Max is controlled by the system policy Average Inclusion Threshold located in Maintain > Policies > Module: Rate of Sale Calculator. It’s recommended that this % of Max is set quite low as it is only supposed to catch the most extreme outliers; default value is 3% or .03.

Step 2

After initial outliers have been removed the data is deseasonalized and new sales values are obtained for all variants (where the seasonal effects have been stripped out). Period totals are used for deseasonalization, with outlier period totals being determined by calculating the median and defining the acceptable deviation from that median in terms of a percentage value. 

This percentage is controlled by the system policy Median Period Total Threshold located in Maintain > Policies > Module: Rate of Sale Calculator. Essentially, it quantifies the max effect you expect seasonal fluctuations to have on sales data, for example, if the % of median=30% you don’t expect your sales to fall below 30% of what is considered the average because of seasonal fluctuations. The default is 50%.

Any periods that fall below this defined level are then scaled back up to the acceptable level.

A deseasonalization factor is calculated by dividing each period total by the average period total.

Step 3

Next, any data points that might still skew the average sales value for a variant after deseasonalization are removed. An average sales value for each variant is calculated and all data points that fall below a specified percentage of that average are eliminated. This percentage is controlled by the system policy Active Variant Threshold Factor located in Maintain > Policies > Module: Rate of Sale Calculator.

Step 4

The final step is to remove any variants that do not have sufficient data to calculate a reliable average. After all outlier data points have been removed the weeks of data for each variant is counted.  All variants with a count that is less than a specified percentage of the total weeks of data are eliminated.  For example if your time period is 9 weeks and your participation percentage is 30% then all variants that do not have at least 3 data points (i.e. 3 weeks of data) are eliminated from the calculation set. The participation percentage is controlled by the system policy Active Variant Time on Offer Minimum located in Maintain > Policies > Module: Rate of Sale Calculator. The default is set to 31% or .31.
