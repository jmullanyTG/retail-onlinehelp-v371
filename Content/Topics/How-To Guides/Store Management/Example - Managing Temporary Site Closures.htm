Example - Managing Temporary Site Closures

In this example, we will manage the process of temporary closing a store for renovation. Steps in this process include:

  • Identifying the store(s) planned for closure and setting or confirming the store closure dates.
  • Implement post-closure stocking strategy changes.
  • Reviewing impact of changes and making any changes as required.

Instructions

1. Set the site closing and re-opening date.
  1. From the Manage tab, click Site Manager.

  2. In the Items dialog box, select the (All Sites) filter.

  3. In the Choose Fields dialog box, select any additional fields you want to view and click OK. For this example, we will select Site Group and Site Status Temporal fields.

  4. In the site document, choose the Charleston site and click on its row in the grid to view its details. (If the Details pane is hidden, click Details on the toolbar to show it.)

  5. In the Site Status tab, if there is not already an upcoming "Closed" status entry for the site, click New in the Entry toolbar to add a new status.

  6. Enter the following values for the status entry:
    • Site Status = Closed
    • Site Status Attribute = Renovate (No Orders)
    • Status Start Date = select a date at least 30 days in the future.
    • Status End Date = select a date at least 30 days after the Status Start Date.

  7. Click Save in the Entry toolbar to save the new status entry.

 2. Review changes to stock holding projections in Item Manager
  1. From the Manage screen, click Item Manager.
  2. In the Items dialog box, select the Review Temporary Closure Setup filter.
  3. Do net select any additional columns.
  4. Select an item to view the effects of the temporary closure.
  5. In the Details pane, click Stock Holding Projections.

  6. In the Stock Holding Projections toolbar, click Calculate By Day to re-calculate the stock holding projections with the temporary closure. You'll see safety stock dwindle down prior to the closure period, stock and demand set to zero during the closure, and finally stock and demand returning in time for the re-opening.

  7. In the Status Information tab, you may see old replenishment orders in the Recommended Orders area that are scheduled to occur just prior to and/or during the temporary closure.

  8. Click Refresh Item Details in the Details pane toolbar to recalculate order recommendations for the temporary closure. You'll see the new orders are adjusted to take into account the absence of demand during the closure and ensure no new orders are accepted until the renovations are completed.

3. Set post-closure stocking strategy for the store.
  1. From the Manage tab, click Policy.
  2. In the Policy screen, find or create the relevant post-renovation policy. (In this example, we'll create a new policy that increases the minimum safety stock to 3 units.)
    1. In the Policy Task toolbar, click New.
    2. In the Add Policy Task toolbar, enter the following values in the General tab:

      • Name = Store Re-Opening Example

      • Filter = Charleston Renovation: Presentation Min Update
    3. In the Policy tab, click Add Dynamic Policy in the Dynamic Policy area.
    4. Enter the following values for the dynamic policy and click OK:
      • Start Date = The day after the store re-opens.
      • Policy Type = Replace

      • Set Minimum Safety Stock = 3

 3. Review safety stock changes made by the post-closure stocking strategy.
  1. In the Policy grid, select the policy you just created and click Execute in the Policy toolbar.
  2. From the Manage tab, click Item Manager.
  3. If the Inventory Manager document with the Charleston items is not open, click Inventor Management Document in the Item Manager toolbar and select the Review Temporary Closure Setup filter in the Items dialog box.
  4. Select an item in the grid and open its Effective Policy tab in the Details pane.
  5. Click Refresh Item Data in the Details pane toolbar to update the information in the tab.

  6. Scroll the Minimum Safety Stock Units row to the date of the re-opening. Confirm that the minimum unit values change to "3" on the re-opening date and beyond.

Results

Once the store closure dates and post-renovation policies have been set and confirmed for the closing store, JustEnough automatically recalculates replenishment strategies during its nightly processing to adjust for the closure (if they have not been manually recalculated already). It will reduce stock as much as possible while ensuring there is enough stock left at the store to meet demand until the closing date. When the re-opening date draws nearer, JustEnough generates orders for the renovated store to ensure that enough inventory is on hand to meet anticipated demand as well as any new inventory policies when the doors reopen.

You can review stock holding projections for store items in the Item Manager to review the impact of the changes you made during this process.
